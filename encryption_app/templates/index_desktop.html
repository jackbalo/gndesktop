The PlayFair Cipher

Battle-Proven Encryption Since WW1

Originally deployed by British Naval Intelligence during World War I, the Playfair Cipher secured critical battlefield communications through its unique digraph substitution system.

Key Historical Uses:
  • Trench communication encryption
  • Royal Navy dispatches
  • Adopted by various armed forces

How It Works

Follow these steps to encrypt your messages:

  1. Enter your plaintext message.
  2. Provide the encryption key (12 characters, letters only).
  3. The system generates encoded text in groups of 5.
Remember: Never share your encryption keys online!
